Male pattern baldness has become a common hair concern affecting a large part of the population. About 25% of men with this condition begin experiencing hair loss before the age of 21. As they age, their hair density gradually decreases, leaving them with significantly thinner hair in their 50s.

Knowing the difference between temporary, reversible hair loss and hair loss due to male pattern baldness is crucial for early intervention. Timely prevention, care, and medical assistance can help you slow down the effects of this condition and retain a fair volume of your natural hair.

What is
Male Pattern Baldness?

Male pattern baldness is also called androgenic alopecia. It is a hair loss condition with a predictable defining progression in men. A receding hairline and thinning hair at the crown are usually among the first signs of this condition. The hair loss eventually leads to the appearance of bald spots and a significant reduction in hair density.

Symptoms of
Male Pattern Baldness?

As mentioned above, hair male pattern baldness has some defining characteristics. Identifying these male pattern hair loss symptoms early on can help you manage the condition effectively. This section discusses the telltale signs of androgenic alopecia at different stages.

Progression and Stages

As pattern baldness in males progresses further, the loss of hair becomes more obvious. The hairline recedes and bald spots begin to form. In more advanced stages among men, balding spots become wider and more pronounced and the hair density decreases significantly.

Early Signs

Men with pattern baldness often experience some subtle changes in their hair texture and increased hair shedding early on. These signs then manifest further into significant hair loss. Looking out for such symptoms if you have a genetic predisposition to this condition can help you take proactive measures to address it.

Progression and Stages

A thinning hairline is among the first indicators of balding in males due to this condition. This often leads to the scalp being visible through the hair.

Receding Hairline

With the progression of male pattern baldness, a receding hairline continues to progress, too. The hairline moves further up and backward on the head and often forms an “M” with deep grooves.

Bald Spots

In advanced stages of hair loss by male pattern baldness, you may notice some prominent bald spots on the crown. The hair along the sides and the back of the head usually remain intact and unaffected.

Causes of Male Pattern Baldness?

To get the right male baldness treatment, you must determine the cause of your condition. Is the hair loss temporary? Is it triggered by a temporary factor from your lifestyle or diet? Knowing the answer to this makes dealing with this hair concern a lot easier.

Genetic Factors

Genetics is among the key factors in causing male pattern baldness. If someone in your close family experiences male pattern baldness symptoms, you are more prone to it than someone without a family history with the condition.

Hormonal Influences

Frequent or significant hormonal changes in your body, especially those involving dihydrotestosterone (DHT) affect your hair follicles and contribute to hair loss. Hormonal imbalances can hold back the production of new hair follicles and optimum supply of nutrients to them. Some hormones like DHT even cause them to shrink and weaken, causing the associated hair strands to fall off prematurely.

Lifestyle and Environmental Factors

Small habits and choices you make daily greatly affect your bodily systems. Your hectic lifestyle and the environment you live in can worsen your hair loss, too. Factors such as chronic stress, improper diet, pollution, and others can be key triggers for men’s balding.

Diagnosis and Assessment

Before addressing male pattern baldness, it is crucial to make a proper diagnosis and rule out other potential hair conditions. Diagnosis of hair male pattern baldness involves noting and recognizing the symptoms and confirming the condition through professional assessment.

This section offers you some insight into the assessment and diagnosis of this hair concern.

How to Identify Symptoms

Observe your hair health and take notes on any changes in your hair’s texture, density, or hairline. During the early phase of the condition, hair loss mostly occurs along the hairline and on the crown of your head. Notice whether you are losing more hair than normal lately and which part of your scalp is losing it the most.

Professional Diagnosis Methods

A hair expert or a dermatologist can assess your hair health and symptoms and diagnose male pattern baldness. Their assessment may involve a physical examination of your hair and scalp. Sometimes, additional tests like hormone level assessment, scalp biopsies, and blood tests can be necessary to rule out other conditions causing hair fall.

Treatment options for
Male Pattern Baldness?

Several male baldness treatments are available today. While some aim to slow down the hair loss, others focus on regenerating lost hair. From topical and oral medications to surgical and non-surgical interventions, you have a plethora of services at your fingertips.

Medical Treatments

Medications like minoxidil and finasteride are commonly used to manage pattern baldness in men and help restore hair density. Several other agents aid in the same and your doctor will suggest the best treatment combination based on your symptoms and hair health and type.

Surgical and Non-Surgical Remedies

In more advanced cases of male pattern baldness thinning hair, surgical and non-surgical interventions can be highly effective. Surgical options like hair transplants and non-surgical remedies like laser therapy, platelet-rich plasma (PRP), and others are an apt choice to help manage your androgenic areata hair loss.

Managing and Preventing
Further Hair Loss

Taking proper care of your hair and scalp is essential to maintain hair health and keep your hair density steady. Avoid known triggers for hair loss like stress, disturbed sleep cycle, processed foods, and others. Instead, be conscious of your meals and physical activity and get medical assistance when you see the first signs of men’s balding due to male pattern baldness.
